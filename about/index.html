About me

I'm Raghav Chhetri. I'm currently a research scientist in the Keller lab at HHMI Janelia Research Campus in Ashburn, VA, and I specialize in designing optical imaging systems to image:

1. the development of early embryos
2. the neural activities in embryos and larvae

Before joining Janelia, I was a Physics PhD student in the Coherence Imaging Laboratory in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at UNC Chapel Hill. While there, I designed and built various OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography) based bio-imaging systems to study:

1. light scattering from plasmonic nanoparticles in complex fluids and extracellular matrix mimics
2. elasticity of excised tissue using superparamagnetic nanoparticles as force transducers
3. viscoelasticity of complex fluids and extracellular matrix mimics
