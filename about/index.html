About

I'm Raghav Chhetri. I'm a research scientist in the Keller lab at HHMI Janelia Research Campus in Ashburn, VA. I design optical imaging systems to study 1. the development of early embryos, and 2. the emergence of their early nervous systems.

Before joining Janelia, I was a PhD student in the ,a href="https://users.physics.unc.edu/~aold/">Coherence Imaging Laboratory in the Department of Physics at UNC Chapel Hill. While there, I designed and built various OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography) based bio-imaging systems to study 1> dynamic light scattering from plasmonic nanoparticles in complex media and extracellular matrix mimics, 2> the viscoelasticity of complex fluids, and 3> the elasticity of excised tissue using superparamagnetic nanoparticles as force transducers.
