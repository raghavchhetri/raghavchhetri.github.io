About me

I'm Raghav Chhetri. I'm currently a research scientist in the Keller lab at HHMI Janelia Research Campus in Ashburn, VA. I design optical imaging systems to study:
1. the development of early embryos
2. the emergence of early nervous systems.

Before joining Janelia, I was a Physics PhD student in the Coherence Imaging Laboratory in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at UNC Chapel Hill. While there, I designed and built various OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography) based bio-imaging systems to study:
1. the light scattering from plasmonic nanoparticles in complex media and extracellular matrix mimics
2. the viscoelasticity of complex fluids
3. the elasticity of excised tissue using superparamagnetic nanoparticles as force transducers
